# Best Free Project Management Tools in 2026 (Real Limits Compared)

Every project management tool advertises a "free plan." But free plans are not created equal. Some give you unlimited everything; others cripple core features until you upgrade. We tested the 10 most popular options and compared their real limits.

## What "Free" Actually Means

Before diving in, let's define what we're measuring:

- **Unlimited tasks/cards**: Can you create as many work items as you need?
- **Member limits**: How many people can use the free plan?
- **Board/project limits**: How many active projects can you have?
- **Storage**: How much file storage do you get?
- **Views**: Do you get access to different views (kanban, list, calendar, Gantt)?
- **Integrations**: How many third-party apps can you connect?

## The Rankings

### 1. ClickUp — Best Overall Free Plan

ClickUp's free plan is the most generous in the category. It's genuinely usable for small teams without feeling like a trial.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited tasks and members
- 100MB storage
- Kanban, list, and calendar views
- Real-time chat
- 24/7 support (yes, even on free)

**Limits:**
- No Gantt/timeline view
- No custom fields
- No time tracking
- Limited automation (100 actions/month)

**Best for**: Small teams (under 10) that need a full-featured PM tool without paying.

### 2. Linear — Best for Engineering Teams

Linear is the darling of the developer community. Its free plan is surprisingly capable for small engineering teams.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited members
- 2 teams
- 250 issues
- Agent platform (AI-powered)
- Keyboard-first interface

**Limits:**
- 250 issues total (not per project — total)
- 2 teams maximum
- No guest access
- No Triage feature

**Best for**: Small engineering teams (under 5 people) who value speed and keyboard-driven workflows.

**Note**: The 250-issue limit is the main constraint. For active projects, you'll hit this quickly. Linear's paid plan ($8/user/month) removes this limit.

[Explore Linear alternatives →](/alternative-to/linear/)

### 3. Trello — Best for Visual Thinkers

Trello pioneered the kanban board approach and remains the simplest tool on this list. If you want a visual board with cards, Trello does it better than anyone.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited cards
- Up to 10 boards per workspace
- Up to 10 collaborators per workspace
- Unlimited storage (10MB per file)
- 1 Power-Up per board

**Limits:**
- 10 boards per workspace
- 10 collaborators
- 1 integration per board
- No custom fields
- No timeline/calendar view

**Best for**: Individuals and very small teams who want a simple, visual way to track tasks.

[Explore Trello alternatives →](/alternative-to/trello/)

### 4. Notion — Best for Flexibility

Notion isn't a traditional project management tool — it's a workspace that can be shaped into one. Its databases, views, and templates make it incredibly flexible.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited pages and blocks
- 10 guest collaborators
- Sync across devices
- 7-day page history
- Database views (table, board, timeline, calendar, gallery, list)

**Limits:**
- 10 guest collaborators
- 7-day version history
- 5MB file upload limit
- No SAML SSO
- No advanced permissions

**Best for**: Individuals and small teams who want a single workspace for notes, docs, and project tracking.

[Explore Notion alternatives →](/alternative-to/notion/)

### 5. Asana — Best for Cross-Functional Teams

Asana is one of the most established PM tools. Its free plan is solid for basic project management but limits advanced features.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited tasks, projects, and messages
- Unlimited storage (100MB per file)
- List, board, and calendar views
- Assignees and due dates
- Basic integrations

**Limits:**
- Up to 10 team members
- No timeline/Gantt view
- No custom fields
- No portfolios
- No milestones
- Limited reporting

**Best for**: Small cross-functional teams (marketing, ops) that need task assignments and deadlines.

[Explore Asana alternatives →](/alternative-to/asana/)

### 6. GitHub Issues — Best for Developers

If your team already uses GitHub, GitHub Issues is a surprisingly capable PM tool — and it's completely free.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited issues and milestones
- Labels, assignees, and projects
- Kanban board view (Projects)
- Automation with GitHub Actions
- Integration with PRs and code

**Limits:**
- No time tracking
- No Gantt view (basic timeline in Projects)
- Limited reporting
- Requires GitHub account

**Best for**: Development teams that want PM integrated directly into their code workflow.

### 7. Plane — Best Open Source Alternative

Plane is the open source alternative to Linear. It offers a similar clean interface with issues, cycles (sprints), and modules.

**Free plan includes:**
- Unlimited members (self-hosted)
- Issues, cycles, and modules
- Kanban and list views
- Basic analytics

**Limits:**
- Cloud plan has member limits
- Fewer integrations than established tools
- Smaller community

**Best for**: Teams that want Linear's UX without the subscription. Self-host for unlimited everything.

## How to Choose

**"I want the most features for free"**: ClickUp — unlimited tasks, members, and the most views on a free plan.

**"I'm an engineering team"**: Linear (if under 250 issues) or GitHub Issues (if you want zero extra tools).

**"I want something dead simple"**: Trello — if you can use a sticky note, you can use Trello.

**"I want one tool for everything"**: Notion — notes, docs, databases, and PM in one workspace.

**"I want to self-host"**: Plane — open source, Linear-like UX, unlimited everything on your own server.
