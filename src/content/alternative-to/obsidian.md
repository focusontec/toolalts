## Why Look for Obsidian Alternatives?

Obsidian is excellent for personal knowledge management, but it has limitations:

- **No real-time collaboration** — Obsidian is designed for single users. Team collaboration requires workarounds.
- **Sync costs** — Obsidian Sync costs $5/month. Free sync options (iCloud, Git) are less reliable.
- **Learning curve** — Markdown-first approach and plugin configuration can overwhelm non-technical users.
- **No web version** — Obsidian is a desktop/mobile app with no browser-based editor.

## Top 5 Obsidian Alternatives

### 1. Logseq — Best Open-Source Alternative

Logseq is an open-source outliner that stores notes locally as Markdown/Org-mode files. It offers bidirectional links, a graph view, and a block-based editing approach.

**Best for:** Users who prefer outliner-style note-taking with local-first storage.

### 2. Notion — Best for Teams

Notion is the best choice if you need real-time collaboration. It offers databases, kanban boards, and rich text editing in a cloud-based workspace.

**Best for:** Teams that need shared workspaces and real-time editing.

### 3. Anytype — Best for Privacy

Anytype offers Notion-like features with peer-to-peer sync and local-first storage. No central servers required.

**Best for:** Privacy-conscious users who want Notion-like features.

### 4. Capacities — Best for Object-Based Notes

Capacities organizes notes around objects (people, books, projects) rather than folders. It's a modern take on personal knowledge management.

**Best for:** Users who think in terms of objects and relationships.

### 5. Apple Notes — Best Simplicity

Apple Notes is free, syncs across Apple devices, and requires zero configuration. It's the simplest option for Apple users.

**Best for:** Apple users who want zero-friction note-taking.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I migrate my Obsidian notes to Logseq?**
A: Yes. Logseq reads the same Markdown files. Just point Logseq at your Obsidian vault folder.

**Q: Which alternative has the best collaboration?**
A: Notion is the clear winner for real-time collaboration.

**Q: Which is best for privacy?**
A: Anytype offers the best privacy with peer-to-peer sync and no central servers. Logseq is also fully local.
