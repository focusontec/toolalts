## Why Look for Slack Alternatives?

Slack is the most popular team chat tool, but it has significant drawbacks:

- **Pricing** — $8.75/user/month for Pro. Enterprise plans are significantly more. Costs scale linearly with team size.
- **Message history limits** — Free plan limits to 90 days of history. Even paid plans have storage limits.
- **Resource heavy** — Slack's Electron app consumes significant RAM and CPU.
- **Vendor lock-in** — Exporting Slack data produces JSON that's hard to migrate elsewhere.

## Top 5 Slack Alternatives

### 1. Rocket.Chat — Best Self-Hosted Alternative

Rocket.Chat is the leading open-source team chat platform. It supports channels, direct messages, file sharing, video calls, and extensive integrations. Self-host it for full data control.

| Feature | Slack | Rocket.Chat |
|---|---|---|
| Open Source | No | Yes |
| Self-Hosted | No | Yes |
| Message History | Limited | Unlimited |
| Video Calls | Huddles | Built-in |
| Pricing | $8.75/user/mo | Free / $2/user |

**Best for:** Teams that want full control over their chat data.

### 2. Mattermost — Best for Dev Teams

Mattermost is an open-source Slack alternative designed for technical teams. It offers deep integrations with DevOps tools, compliance features, and self-hosting options.

**Best for:** Engineering teams that need DevOps integrations.

### 3. Discord — Best for Communities

Discord offers unlimited message history, voice channels, and excellent community features — all free. It's increasingly used by startups and open-source projects.

**Best for:** Communities, startups, and teams that want free unlimited chat.

### 4. Zulip — Best for Organized Conversations

Zulip uses a unique topic-based threading model that keeps conversations organized. It's open-source and offers both cloud and self-hosted options.

**Best for:** Teams that struggle with Slack's linear channel model.

### 5. Element (Matrix) — Best for Privacy

Element is built on the Matrix protocol, offering end-to-end encrypted, decentralized communication. It's ideal for privacy-conscious organizations.

**Best for:** Organizations with strict privacy and compliance requirements.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I migrate my Slack data to Rocket.Chat?**
A: Yes. Rocket.Chat has a built-in Slack importer that transfers channels, messages, and users. Private channels require a Slack Business+ export.

**Q: Which alternative is truly free for unlimited users?**
A: Discord and Rocket.Chat (self-hosted) are free for unlimited users with unlimited message history.

**Q: Which is best for developer teams?**
A: Mattermost is designed for developer teams with deep integrations for GitHub, GitLab, Jira, and CI/CD tools.
