## Overview

Cursor is a code editor with native AI integration designed to make developers extraordinarily productive. It functions as a pair-programming assistant, offering cloud agents, Bugbot, and enterprise-grade security features like SAML/OIDC SSO. With a stellar 4.7/5 rating from over 320 reviews, Cursor is a polished, closed-source tool that prioritizes ease of use and powerful AI capabilities out of the box.

Dyad, on the other hand, is an open-source AI app builder that emphasizes flexibility and local execution. It allows users to build AI applications using their preferred models and tools, avoiding vendor lock-in. With 200 GitHub stars and a 3.5/5 rating (based on limited reviews), Dyad is a newer, community-driven alternative that appeals to developers who want full control over their AI workflow.

## Pricing

**Cursor** offers four tiers:
- **Hobby (Free):** Basic AI features, limited usage.
- **Pro ($20/month):** Unlimited AI usage, cloud agents, Bugbot.
- **Teams ($40/user/month):** All Pro features plus SAML/OIDC SSO, team management.
- **Enterprise (Custom):** Tailored solutions for large organizations.

**Dyad** has three tiers:
- **Dyad Free (Free):** 200 AI credits/month, basic features.
- **Dyad Pro ($20/month):** Unlimited AI credits, exclusive Pro modes for large codebases.
- **Dyad Max ($79/month):** All Pro features plus priority support and advanced capabilities.

## When to Choose Cursor

Choose Cursor if you are a professional developer or part of a team that values a polished, ready-to-use AI coding assistant. Cursor excels in scenarios where:

- **You need enterprise-grade security:** SAML/OIDC SSO and Teams plans make it ideal for organizations with compliance requirements.
- **You want a proven tool:** With a 4.7/5 rating and 320 reviews, Cursor has a strong track record of user satisfaction.
- **You work on large projects:** Cloud agents and Bugbot help manage complex codebases efficiently.
- **You prefer a cloud-based solution:** No local setup required; everything works out of the box.

Cursor is best for developers who want to boost productivity without worrying about infrastructure or model management.

## When to Choose Dyad

Choose Dyad if you value open-source flexibility and local execution. Dyad is ideal for:

- **Avoiding vendor lock-in:** You can use your preferred AI models and tools, and all data stays local.
- **Building custom AI applications:** Dyad is an app builder, not just a code editor, giving you more control over your AI workflow.
- **Working offline or in sensitive environments:** Local execution ensures data privacy and independence from cloud services.
- **Experimenting with AI:** The free tier (200 credits/month) and open-source nature make it great for learning and prototyping.

Dyad is best for developers who prioritize control, privacy, and customization over convenience.

## Verdict

Both tools serve different needs in the AI development space. Cursor is the clear winner for professional teams and enterprises seeking a reliable, feature-rich AI coding assistant with strong support and security. Its high rating and comprehensive feature set make it a safe choice for boosting productivity.

Dyad, while newer and less proven, offers a compelling alternative for developers who want to build AI applications locally without lock-in. Its open-source nature and flexible pricing make it attractive for hobbyists, privacy-conscious users, and those who enjoy tinkering.

**Recommendation:** If you need a turnkey AI coding assistant for daily development, go with Cursor. If you want to build custom AI applications with full control over your data and models, start with Dyad. For most professional developers, Cursor is the more practical choice today, but Dyad is worth watching as it matures.
