## Overview

Cursor and Stagewise are both AI-powered development tools, but they approach the problem from different angles. Cursor is a polished, commercial code editor that integrates AI directly into the coding experience, aiming to boost individual and team productivity through features like cloud agents and Bugbot. Stagewise, on the other hand, is an open-source IDE focused on creating and orchestrating coding agents, with support for multiple AI models from various providers. While Cursor emphasizes a seamless, out-of-the-box experience for pair-programming, Stagewise offers flexibility and extensibility for developers who want to control their AI workflows.

## Pricing

**Cursor** offers a tiered pricing model:
- **Hobby (Free)**: Basic AI features, limited usage
- **Pro ($20/month)**: Full AI capabilities, cloud agents, Bugbot
- **Teams ($40/user/month)**: All Pro features plus SAML/OIDC SSO, team management
- **Enterprise (Custom)**: Custom pricing for large organizations with dedicated support

**Stagewise** is completely free:
- **Free ($0)**: All features included, open source, no usage limits

## When to Choose Cursor

Cursor is the better choice if you prioritize a polished, production-ready experience with minimal setup. Its native AI integration is designed for pair-programming, making it ideal for developers who want AI assistance without managing complex configurations. The cloud agents and Bugbot features are particularly valuable for teams looking to automate repetitive tasks and catch bugs early. Cursor’s SSO support (SAML/OIDC) also makes it a strong fit for enterprises that require security and compliance. With a 4.7/5 rating from 320 reviews, Cursor has a proven track record of user satisfaction.

## When to Choose Stagewise

Stagewise is the better choice if you value flexibility, control, and cost savings. As an open-source IDE, it allows you to create and orchestrate custom coding agents tailored to your specific workflows. Its multi-model support means you can switch between AI providers (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, local models) without being locked into a single ecosystem. Stagewise is also ideal for developers who need app previews and git workflow integration built directly into the IDE. If you’re a solo developer or a small team on a tight budget, the free pricing is a significant advantage. However, keep in mind that Stagewise has no user reviews yet and a smaller community (300 GitHub stars), so support and stability may be limited.

## Verdict

Both tools serve different needs in the AI-assisted development space. Cursor is the best choice for teams and enterprises that want a reliable, feature-rich, and secure AI coding assistant with minimal friction. Its pricing is reasonable for the value it provides, especially for Pro and Teams users. Stagewise, meanwhile, is a compelling option for developers who want to experiment with multiple AI models, build custom agent workflows, and avoid vendor lock-in—all at zero cost. However, its lack of reviews and smaller community mean it’s better suited for early adopters and tinkerers. If you need a battle-tested tool for daily production work, go with Cursor. If you prioritize openness and flexibility, give Stagewise a try.
