## Overview

This memo compares Discord and Zoom as communication tools for a real buyer deciding which platform best fits their team's workflow. Discord is positioned as a community and gaming-oriented platform with persistent voice channels and server-based organization, while Zoom is an enterprise-focused collaboration suite with structured meetings, AI features, and integrated productivity tools. The decision hinges on whether your team needs always-on, informal communication (Discord) or scheduled, feature-rich meetings with advanced administration (Zoom).

## Key Differences

1. **Communication Model**: Discord uses persistent voice channels and text channels organized into servers—ideal for ongoing, drop-in conversations. Zoom is built around scheduled meetings with a clear start and end time, plus chat as a secondary feature.

2. **Meeting Duration Limits**: Zoom's free Basic plan caps meetings at 40 minutes, while Discord's free plan has no time limit on voice or video calls. This is a critical constraint for longer discussions on Zoom's free tier.

3. **Participant Capacity**: Zoom supports up to 100 participants on free and Pro plans, scaling to 1,000 on Enterprise. Discord's participant limits for voice/video are not specified in the provided data—this is an evidence gap.

4. **AI and Productivity Features**: Zoom includes AI note-taking, meeting summaries, AI queries, and workflows across its paid plans. Discord offers no AI features in the provided evidence.

5. **Administration and Security**: Zoom Business and Enterprise include SSO, managed domains, device management, and archival APIs. Discord's admin capabilities are not detailed in the evidence—this is a gap for enterprise buyers.

## Pricing

- **Discord Free**: $0 – unlimited servers, voice/video, screen share, file sharing.
- **Discord Nitro**: $9.99/month per user – HD video, higher upload limits, custom emoji, server boosts.
- **Zoom Basic**: Free – 40-minute meetings, 100 participants, limited AI features (3 meeting summaries/month, 20 AI queries/month, 3 My Notes uses/month).
- **Zoom Pro**: $14.16/user/month – 30-hour meetings, 100 participants, unlimited AI note-taking, 10 GB cloud storage per user.
- **Zoom Business**: $18.33/user/month – 300 participants, unlimited whiteboards, SSO, managed domains, device management.
- **Zoom Enterprise**: Contact for pricing – 1,000 participants, full PBX phone, webinars for 500 attendees, translated captions.

**Note**: Discord's participant limits for voice/video calls are not verified in the evidence. Zoom's Enterprise pricing is not disclosed.

## When to Choose Discord

- **Gaming or hobby communities** that need always-on voice channels for casual drop-in conversations.
- **Small teams or friend groups** that want unlimited free voice/video without time limits.
- **Communities that rely on bots** for moderation, music, or automation—Discord's bot ecosystem is a verified feature.
- **Users who prioritize persistent, channel-based organization** over scheduled meetings and calendar integration.

## When to Choose Zoom

- **Business teams that need structured meetings** with clear agendas, scheduling, and calendar sync.
- **Organizations requiring AI-powered productivity**—Zoom's note-taking, summaries, and AI queries are verified features.
- **Enterprises needing admin controls** like SSO, device management, and data archival (Business+ plans).
- **Teams that host webinars or large meetings**—Zoom supports up to 1,000 participants and 500 webinar attendees on Enterprise.
- **Users who want an all-in-one platform** with integrated phone, mail, calendar, whiteboard, and task management.

## Trade-offs and Limits

- **Evidence gaps**: Discord's participant limits for voice/video calls are not provided. Zoom's free tier has a strict 40-minute meeting cap that may frustrate longer discussions. Discord's admin and security features are not detailed, making enterprise comparison incomplete.
- **Migration friction**: Switching from Discord to Zoom means losing persistent voice channels and bot integrations. Switching from Zoom to Discord means losing AI features, calendar integration, and structured meeting workflows.
- **Pricing model differences**: Discord charges per user at a flat $9.99/month for Nitro, while Zoom charges per user per month with tiered plans. For a team of 10, Discord Nitro costs $99.90/month total, while Zoom Pro costs $141.60/month total.
- **Missing data**: Neither tool's reliability, uptime, or customer support quality is verified in the evidence. Zoom's free tier AI features are heavily limited (3 meeting summaries, 20 AI queries, 3 My Notes uses per month).

## Verdict

- **Choose Discord** if your team is a small, informal group (under 20 people) that values persistent voice channels, free unlimited calls, and bot-driven automation over structured meetings and AI features. It's best for gaming communities, hobby groups, or teams that communicate continuously throughout the day.
- **Choose Zoom** if your team needs scheduled meetings with calendar integration, AI note-taking, and enterprise-grade admin controls. It's the right choice for business teams, organizations with compliance requirements, or anyone who needs to host large meetings or webinars.
- **Avoid switching** if your team is heavily invested in one platform's unique features—Discord's bots or Zoom's AI tools—as the other tool lacks equivalent capabilities.
