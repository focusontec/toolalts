## Overview

Rowboat and Ephe are both open-source productivity tools, but they serve very different purposes. Rowboat is an AI coworker that remembers past interactions and automates repetitive tasks, making it ideal for developers and knowledge workers who need a customizable, self-hosted assistant. Ephe, on the other hand, is a minimal, ephemeral markdown editor focused on quick note-taking and sharing without permanent storage. While Rowboat aims to boost long-term productivity through memory and automation, Ephe prioritizes a distraction-free, transient writing experience.

## Pricing

Both tools are completely free, with no paid tiers or hidden costs.

**Rowboat:** Free ($0 USD per user/month). Includes unlimited public/private repositories, Dependabot security and version updates, 2,000 CI/CD minutes per month, 500MB of Packages storage, Issues & Projects, community support, and GitHub Codespaces access.

**Ephe:** Free ($0). No pricing tiers or premium features. The entire tool is available at no cost, with no storage limits because notes are ephemeral by design.

## When to Choose Rowboat

Choose Rowboat if you need an AI-powered assistant that learns from your past interactions and automates repetitive tasks. It’s ideal for developers and teams who want a self-hosted, customizable coworker that integrates with GitHub workflows. Key scenarios include:

- **Task automation:** Automate routine processes like dependency updates, code reviews, or project management tasks.
- **Team collaboration:** Use Issues & Projects and shared memory to keep everyone aligned.
- **CI/CD pipelines:** Leverage 2,000 free CI/CD minutes per month for testing and deployment.
- **Security-conscious teams:** Dependabot provides automated security and version updates for your repositories.
- **Customization:** As an open-source tool, you can modify Rowboat to fit your specific needs.

Rowboat is best for users who value long-term productivity gains through memory and automation, especially in a development context.

## When to Choose Ephe

Choose Ephe if you want a lightweight, no-frills markdown editor for quick, disposable notes. It’s perfect for plain markdown lovers who need a distraction-free writing experience without worrying about storage or organization. Key scenarios include:

- **Quick note-taking:** Jot down ideas, to-do lists, or meeting notes without committing to permanent storage.
- **Sharing ephemeral content:** Write and share notes that automatically disappear after use.
- **Minimalist workflows:** Avoid feature bloat and focus solely on writing in markdown.
- **Privacy-conscious users:** Since nothing is stored permanently, there’s no risk of data lingering.
- **Lightweight tooling:** Ephe runs locally and requires no setup or account.

Ephe is best for users who prioritize simplicity, speed, and privacy over automation or memory.

## Verdict

Rowboat and Ephe are both excellent open-source tools, but they solve fundamentally different problems. Rowboat is a powerful AI coworker for developers and teams who need automation, memory, and GitHub integration. Ephe is a minimalist markdown editor for quick, ephemeral note-taking.

**Choose Rowboat** if you need a self-hosted AI assistant that learns from your work and automates repetitive tasks. It’s a robust choice for development teams and knowledge workers who want to boost long-term productivity.

**Choose Ephe** if you want a distraction-free, temporary writing tool for quick notes and sharing. It’s ideal for plain markdown enthusiasts who value simplicity and privacy over features.

Both tools are free and open-source, so you can try them without any financial commitment. For most users, the decision comes down to whether you need memory and automation (Rowboat) or ephemeral, lightweight writing (Ephe).
