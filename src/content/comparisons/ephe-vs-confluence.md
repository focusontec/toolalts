## Overview

Ephe is a minimal, ephemeral markdown editor designed for quick note-taking and sharing. It’s an open-source tool (200 GitHub stars) that focuses on a lightweight, no-frills writing experience. Ephe is ideal for users who want to jot down ideas, share snippets, or work with plain markdown without permanent storage. It’s free and open-source, making it a great choice for developers and minimalists.

Confluence, by Atlassian, is a team workspace where knowledge meets collaboration. It’s a robust platform with rich text editing, real-time co-editing, page trees, and deep integrations with Jira and Trello. With a 4.3/5 rating from over 3,800 reviews, Confluence is a mature, enterprise-grade solution for teams that need structured documentation, project management, and collaboration at scale.

## Pricing

**Ephe** is completely free ($0 USD per user/month). It offers unlimited public/private repositories, Dependabot security updates, 2,000 CI/CD minutes/month, 500MB of Packages storage, Issues & Projects, and community support. GitHub Codespaces access is available as a featured add-on.

**Confluence** has four tiers:
- **Free ($0):** Up to 10 users, 2GB storage, basic templates, and limited integrations.
- **Standard ($6.05/user/mo):** Unlimited users, 250GB storage, page insights, and advanced permissions.
- **Premium ($11.55/user/mo):** Unlimited storage, AI-powered search, 24/7 support, and admin analytics.
- **Enterprise (Custom):** Unlimited storage, advanced security, dedicated support, and compliance features.

## When to Choose Ephe

Choose Ephe if you are a developer, writer, or minimalist who needs a lightweight, ephemeral markdown editor for quick notes and sharing. It’s perfect for:
- **Quick note-taking:** No setup, no permanent storage—just write and share.
- **Open-source enthusiasts:** Full control over your data and the ability to self-host.
- **Budget-conscious users:** Completely free with no user limits.
- **GitHub integration:** Use with GitHub Actions, Dependabot, and CI/CD pipelines.
- **Ephemeral content:** Ideal for temporary notes, drafts, or snippets you don’t want to keep.

## When to Choose Confluence

Choose Confluence if you are part of a team or organization that needs structured documentation, collaboration, and project management. It’s best for:
- **Team collaboration:** Real-time co-editing, comments, and page trees for shared knowledge.
- **Enterprise needs:** Granular permissions, compliance, and advanced security.
- **Integration-heavy workflows:** Deep integration with Jira, Trello, and hundreds of other tools.
- **Scalability:** From small teams to large enterprises with custom plans.
- **Rich content:** Templates, macros, and multimedia support for polished documentation.

## Verdict

Ephe and Confluence serve very different purposes. Ephe is a minimal, free, open-source markdown editor for quick, ephemeral notes—ideal for individuals or developers who value simplicity and privacy. Confluence is a powerful, feature-rich team workspace for structured collaboration, documentation, and project management—best for teams and enterprises that need scalability and integration.

**Choose Ephe** if you want a lightweight, free tool for temporary notes and sharing, and you’re comfortable with markdown and GitHub. **Choose Confluence** if you need a robust, collaborative platform for long-term knowledge management, team workflows, and enterprise-grade features. For most teams, Confluence’s depth and ecosystem outweigh Ephe’s simplicity, but for individual developers or minimalists, Ephe is a refreshing alternative.
