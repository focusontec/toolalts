# Notion vs Anki: Workspace vs Flashcard Learning

Notion and Anki are both productivity tools, but they serve fundamentally different purposes. Notion is an all-in-one workspace for notes, docs, and collaboration. Anki is a spaced repetition flashcard system for memorization. This comparison helps you understand when to use each.

## At a Glance

| Feature | Notion | Anki |
|---------|--------|------|
| **Type** | Workspace / Wiki | Spaced repetition flashcards |
| **Pricing** | Free / $8-16/user/mo | Free (open source) |
| **Collaboration** | Real-time multiplayer | Solo learning |
| **Best For** | Notes, docs, project management | Memorization and retention |
| **AI Features** | Writing assistant, summaries | AI flashcard generation |
| **Learning Curve** | Moderate | Moderate |
| **Platforms** | Web, Mac, Windows, iOS, Android | Web, Mac, Windows, Linux, iOS, Android |

## What Each Tool Does

### Notion: The All-in-One Workspace

Notion is a flexible workspace that combines notes, databases, wikis, and project management:

- **Rich text editing** — Write notes with headings, lists, code blocks, and embeds
- **Databases** — Create tables, boards, calendars, and galleries
- **Templates** — Thousands of pre-built templates for any use case
- **Real-time collaboration** — Work with teammates simultaneously
- **API access** — Build custom integrations and automations

Notion is where you organize information, manage projects, and collaborate with others.

### Anki: The Memorization Machine

Anki uses spaced repetition to help you memorize information efficiently:

- **Spaced repetition algorithm** — Shows cards right before you'd forget them
- **Custom card types** — Basic, cloze deletion, image occlusion, and more
- **Shared decks** — Download pre-made decks for languages, medicine, law, etc.
- **Statistics** — Track your learning progress with detailed analytics
- **Cross-platform sync** — Study on any device with AnkiWeb

Anki is where you commit information to long-term memory.

## Different Problems, Different Tools

These tools solve different problems:

**Notion** answers: "How do I organize and access information?"
- Store meeting notes, project docs, and knowledge bases
- Collaborate with team members on shared content
- Manage tasks and projects with databases

**Anki** answers: "How do I remember what I've learned?"
- Memorize vocabulary for language learning
- Study medical terminology and anatomy
- Retain technical concepts for exams

## When to Choose Notion

Pick Notion if you:

- **Need a team workspace** for collaboration
- **Manage complex projects** with databases and timelines
- **Build a knowledge base** or company wiki
- **Want an all-in-one tool** that replaces multiple apps
- **Create and organize content** rather than memorize it

## When to Choose Anki

Pick Anki if you:

- **Study for exams** that require memorization
- **Learn languages** and need to retain vocabulary
- **Master technical concepts** through repetition
- **Want scientifically-proven** learning methods
- **Prefer focused study sessions** over general note-taking

## Using Both Together

Many learners use both tools effectively:

1. **Take notes in Notion** — Capture information, organize concepts, create study guides
2. **Create flashcards in Anki** — Extract key facts and create cards for memorization
3. **Review in Anki** — Use spaced repetition to commit information to memory
4. **Reference in Notion** — Look up details when you need context

This workflow combines Notion's organizational strengths with Anki's memorization power.

## Pricing

### Notion Pricing
- **Free** — Unlimited pages for individuals, 10 guests, 5MB uploads
- **Plus ($8/user/mo)** — Unlimited blocks, 100MB uploads, 30-day history
- **Business ($16/user/mo)** — SAML SSO, advanced permissions
- **Enterprise (Custom)** — Audit log, dedicated support

### Anki Pricing
- **Desktop** — Free and open source
- **AnkiWeb** — Free cloud sync
- **AnkiMobile (iOS)** — $24.99 one-time purchase (supports development)
- **AnkiDroid (Android)** — Free and open source

## The Verdict

**Choose Notion** if you need a workspace for organizing information, collaborating with teams, and managing projects. It's the better tool for knowledge management and productivity.

**Choose Anki** if you need to memorize information efficiently. Its spaced repetition algorithm is scientifically proven to improve retention. It's the better tool for learning and studying.

**Use both** if you want the best of both worlds: Notion for organizing what you learn, Anki for remembering it.
