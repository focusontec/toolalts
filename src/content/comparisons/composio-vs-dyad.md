## Overview

**composio** is a powerful open-source platform designed for AI agents that require just-in-time tool calls, secure delegated authentication, and sandboxed execution environments. With support for over 1,000 apps and parallel execution, it’s built for developers who need high-volume, secure, and scalable tool integrations. Its tagline, “Just-in-time tool calls, secure delegated auth, and sandboxed environments for AI agents,” highlights its focus on enterprise-grade agent infrastructure.

**Dyad** is an open-source AI app builder that prioritizes local execution and flexibility. It allows developers to build AI applications using their preferred models and tools without vendor lock-in. With a tagline of “Flexible, local, open-source AI app builder with zero lock-in,” Dyad is ideal for developers who want to keep their workflows private and customizable, especially for large codebases.

## Pricing

**composio** offers a generous free tier and scalable paid plans:
- **Totally Free** ($0/month): 20K tool calls/month – perfect for experimentation.
- **Ridiculously Cheap** ($29/month): 200K tool calls/month – for growing projects.
- **Serious Business** ($229/month): 2M tool calls/month – for production workloads.
- **For Enterprise**: Custom quote for high-volume or custom needs.

**Dyad** has a simpler, usage-based pricing model:
- **Dyad Free** ($0/month): 200 AI credits/month – basic local building.
- **Dyad Pro** ($20/month): Includes exclusive Pro modes for large codebases.
- **Dyad Max** ($79/month): Highest tier for intensive local development.

## When to Choose composio

Choose composio if you’re building AI agents that need to interact with a wide range of external services securely and at scale. It’s ideal for:
- **High-volume tool calls**: With plans offering up to 2M tool calls/month, composio handles heavy workloads.
- **Secure delegated auth**: Perfect for agents that need to act on behalf of users across many apps.
- **Sandboxed environments**: Essential for running untrusted code or third-party integrations safely.
- **Parallel execution**: Boosts performance for complex agent workflows.
- **Enterprise readiness**: The “Serious Business” plan and custom enterprise options make it suitable for large teams.

If your project involves integrating dozens of APIs, managing user permissions, or running agents in production, composio’s robust infrastructure and high GitHub star count (28K+) signal a mature, community-backed solution.

## When to Choose Dyad

Choose Dyad if you prioritize local control, privacy, and flexibility over cloud-based scaling. It’s best for:
- **Local execution**: Keep all data and processing on your machine, avoiding cloud dependencies.
- **Zero lock-in**: Use any AI model or tool you prefer without being tied to a specific provider.
- **Large codebase work**: The Pro mode is tailored for developers working on complex, local projects.
- **Lightweight needs**: The free tier (200 credits/month) is sufficient for small experiments or personal use.
- **Simplicity**: Dyad’s straightforward pricing and local focus appeal to developers who want a no-fuss builder.

If you’re a solo developer or small team building AI apps that don’t require extensive external integrations, Dyad’s local-first approach and low cost ($20–$79/month) are attractive.

## Verdict

Both composio and Dyad are open-source tools in the development category, but they serve different niches. composio excels as a high-performance, cloud-based agent infrastructure for integrating with hundreds of apps securely and at scale. Its generous free tier and scalable pricing make it a strong choice for teams building production-ready AI agents. Dyad, on the other hand, is a lightweight, local-first app builder that prioritizes privacy and flexibility, ideal for developers who want to avoid vendor lock-in and work offline.

**Recommendation**: If you need to build AI agents that interact with many external services and require robust security and scalability, go with composio. If you prefer a local, customizable AI app builder for personal or small-scale projects, choose Dyad. For most production use cases, composio’s feature set and community support (28K+ stars) give it a clear edge.
