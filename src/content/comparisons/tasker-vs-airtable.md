## Overview

Tasker and Airtable both aim to boost productivity, but they tackle the problem from opposite ends. Tasker is a free, open-source desktop app that uses AI to automate any website. You can record a workflow or simply describe it in plain English, and the AI executes the steps across Windows, Mac, or Linux. Airtable, on the other hand, is a low-code platform for building collaborative apps. It combines the familiarity of a spreadsheet with the power of a database, letting teams create custom solutions for project management, CRM, inventory, and more. While Tasker focuses on automating repetitive browser tasks, Airtable excels at organizing, tracking, and collaborating on structured data.

## Pricing

**Tasker** is completely free. There are no tiers, no hidden costs, and no subscription required. You download the open-source desktop app and use it indefinitely at $0.

**Airtable** offers four pricing tiers:
- **Free**: Unlimited bases, but limited to 1,000 records per base, 1 GB attachment space, and 100 automation runs per month.
- **Team**: $20/seat/month (billed annually). Includes 50,000 records per base, 5 GB attachment space, 25,000 automation runs, and extensions.
- **Business**: $45/seat/month (billed annually). Adds 500,000 records per base, 20 GB attachment space, 100,000 automation runs, SAML SSO, admin panel, and audit logs.
- **Enterprise Scale**: Custom pricing for advanced security, dedicated support, and enterprise API access.

## When to Choose Tasker

Choose Tasker if your primary need is automating repetitive browser tasks without writing code. It’s ideal for:
- **Web scraping** – Automatically extract data from websites by recording a workflow or describing it in natural language.
- **Form filling** – Automate logging into portals, filling out forms, or submitting data across multiple sites.
- **Cross-platform users** – Tasker runs on Windows, Mac, and Linux, making it a universal tool for any desktop environment.
- **Budget-conscious teams** – Since it’s free and open-source, you can use it without any financial commitment. Developers can also fork and customize the code.
- **Quick one-off automations** – If you need to automate a task today and don’t want to learn a complex tool, Tasker’s plain-English interface gets you there fast.

Tasker is not a database or collaboration platform. It’s a focused automation tool for browser-based workflows.

## When to Choose Airtable

Choose Airtable when you need a structured, collaborative database that your whole team can use. It’s the better choice for:
- **Project management** – Track tasks, deadlines, dependencies, and assignees with Gantt, Timeline, or Kanban views.
- **CRM and sales pipelines** – Manage contacts, deals, and follow-ups with custom fields and automation.
- **Inventory and asset tracking** – Store up to 500,000 records per base with attachments, rich text, and linked records.
- **Team collaboration** – Multiple editors can work simultaneously, with permissions, audit logs, and version history.
- **Building custom apps** – Use Interface Designer and Extensions to create tailored dashboards and tools without coding.
- **Enterprise needs** – SAML SSO, admin panel, audit logs, and DLP features make Airtable suitable for regulated industries.

Airtable is not a browser automation tool. It won’t fill web forms or scrape sites for you.

## Verdict

Tasker and Airtable serve fundamentally different purposes, so the “winner” depends entirely on your use case. If you need to automate repetitive browser tasks—like scraping data, filling forms, or logging into multiple sites—Tasker is a powerful, free, and open-source solution that gets the job done with minimal effort. It’s perfect for individuals or small teams who want to eliminate manual web work.

If your goal is to build a collaborative database, manage projects, or create custom apps for your team, Airtable is the industry leader. Its rich feature set, scalability, and strong user satisfaction (4.6/5 from 2,200 reviews) make it a reliable choice for businesses of all sizes. However, its cost can add up quickly for larger teams.

For many users, the best approach is to use both: Tasker for automating web tasks that feed data into Airtable, and Airtable for organizing and collaborating on that data. Together, they form a powerful productivity stack.
