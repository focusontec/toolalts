## Overview

Notion and Airtable are two of the most popular productivity platforms on the market, but they take fundamentally different approaches to getting work done. Notion positions itself as an all-in-one AI workspace where teams can write, plan, and collaborate—with powerful AI agents and automation baked in. Airtable, on the other hand, is a low-code platform designed for building custom collaborative apps, with a strong emphasis on relational databases and visual interfaces.

Both tools boast excellent user ratings—Notion at 4.7/5 from 5,800 reviews, and Airtable at 4.6/5 from 2,200 reviews—but they serve different primary use cases. Notion excels as a flexible knowledge management and project collaboration hub, while Airtable shines when you need to build structured, scalable applications without writing code.

## Feature Comparison

| **Core functionality** | AI workspace with docs, wikis, and databases | Low-code platform for building collaborative apps |

| **Open source** | No | No |

## Pricing

**Notion Pricing:**
- **Free:** $0 per member/month – Unlimited pages, blocks, and file uploads; 7-day page history; basic integrations
- **Plus:** $10 per member/month – Unlimited file uploads, 30-day page history, custom automations, AI features
- **Business:** $20 per member/month – SAML SSO, private teamspaces, 90-day page history, bulk PDF export
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing – User provisioning (SCIM), audit log, customer success manager, security compliance connections

**Airtable Pricing:**
- **Free:** $0 – Unlimited bases, 1,000 records per base, 1 GB attachment space, basic views
- **Team:** $20/seat/month (billed annually) – 50,000 records per base, 5 GB attachment space, 1,000 automation runs/month, Gantt & timeline views
- **Business:** $45/seat/month (billed annually) – 500,000 records per base, 20 GB attachment space, 50,000 automation runs/month, SAML SSO, admin panel, audit logs
- **Enterprise Scale:** Custom pricing – Enterprise API, advanced admin controls, dedicated support

## When to Choose Notion

Choose Notion if your team needs a flexible, AI-powered workspace that combines documentation, project management, and knowledge management in one place. Notion is ideal for:

- **Teams that write and collaborate heavily** – Notion's rich text editor, wikis, and meeting notes make it perfect for documentation-heavy workflows.
- **AI-first workflows** – Notion's AI capabilities (Notion Agent, AI Meeting Notes, custom agents) are more advanced and integrated than Airtable's basic AI credits.
- **Small to medium teams on a budget** – At $10/member/month for Plus, Notion is significantly cheaper than Airtable's Team plan ($20/seat/month).
- **Flexible, unstructured data** – Notion's databases are more forgiving and easier to set up for general-purpose project tracking, content calendars, and knowledge bases.
- **Enterprise security needs** – Notion offers zero data retention with LLM providers, SCIM provisioning, and DLP/SIEM integrations out of the box.

## When to Choose Airtable

Choose Airtable if your team needs to build structured, scalable applications with complex relational data and custom interfaces. Airtable is ideal for:

- **Data-heavy operations** – Airtable handles up to 500,000 records per base with robust relational database features, making it better for CRM, inventory management, or asset tracking.
- **Low-code app building** – Airtable's Interface Designer and Extensions let you build custom apps without coding, which is more powerful than Notion's basic forms and sites.
- **Visual project management** – Airtable's Gantt and timeline views are more mature and feature-rich than Notion's equivalents.
- **Teams that need strict data structure** – If you need defined field types, linked records, and formula fields, Airtable's schema is more rigorous and reliable.
- **Larger teams with complex workflows** – Airtable's Business plan ($45/seat/month) offers 50,000 automation runs/month and 500,000 records per base, suitable for enterprise-scale operations.

## Verdict

Both Notion and Airtable are excellent tools, but they serve different primary needs. **Notion is the better choice for teams that want an all-in-one AI workspace for collaboration, documentation, and lightweight project management**—especially if you value AI features and lower cost. **Airtable is the better choice for teams that need to build custom, data-driven applications with complex relational databases and visual interfaces**—especially if you need scalability and structured data management.

For most small to medium teams, Notion offers better value and flexibility. For teams with heavy data requirements or those building internal tools, Airtable's low-code platform is worth the higher price. Consider starting with Notion for general productivity and migrating to Airtable if your data needs outgrow Notion's capabilities.
