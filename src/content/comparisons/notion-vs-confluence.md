## Overview

Notion and Confluence are two leading productivity platforms that help teams organize knowledge, manage projects, and collaborate effectively. Notion positions itself as an AI-powered workspace that adapts to your workflow, offering databases, custom agents, and automation. Confluence, built by Atlassian, focuses on structured knowledge management with deep integration into the Jira ecosystem, making it a staple for technical teams. Notion holds a 4.7/5 rating from 5,800 reviews, while Confluence scores 4.3/5 from 3,800 reviews.

## Pricing

**Notion Pricing:**
- **Free:** $0 per member/month – Unlimited pages, blocks, and file uploads (5MB limit)
- **Plus:** $10 per member/month – Unlimited file uploads, custom properties, 30-day page history
- **Business:** $20 per member/month – SAML SSO, private teamspaces, domain verification, 90-day page history
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing – User provisioning (SCIM), audit log, customer success manager, security compliance connections

**Confluence Pricing:**
- **Free:** $0 – Up to 10 users, 2GB storage, basic templates
- **Standard:** $6.05/user/month – Unlimited users, 250GB storage, page insights
- **Premium:** $11.55/user/month – AI features (add-on), 24/7 support, 99.9% uptime SLA
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing – Unlimited storage, advanced admin controls, dedicated support

## When to Choose Notion

Choose Notion if you need a flexible, all-in-one workspace that goes beyond documentation. Notion excels for teams that want to build custom databases with subtasks, dependencies, and custom properties—ideal for project management, CRM, or content planning. Its AI capabilities, including Notion Agent and AI Meeting Notes, automate busywork and search across all your apps. Notion is also better for non-technical teams that need custom forms, sites, and unlimited charts without coding. The Business tier ($20/user/month) offers SAML SSO, private teamspaces, and domain verification, making it suitable for growing companies. If you value zero data retention with LLM providers and granular permissions, Notion’s Enterprise plan provides advanced security and compliance connections (DLP, SIEM).

## When to Choose Confluence

Choose Confluence if your team is deeply embedded in the Atlassian ecosystem, especially with Jira and Trello. Confluence’s page trees and rich text editor with templates make it perfect for structured documentation like product specs, technical guides, and meeting notes. Real-time co-editing and inline comments streamline feedback loops. Confluence is more affordable at the Standard tier ($6.05/user/month) compared to Notion’s Plus ($10/user/month), making it budget-friendly for larger teams. It also offers team calendars and content permissions out of the box. For technical teams that prioritize hierarchical organization and Jira integration over AI and databases, Confluence is the natural choice. The Premium tier ($11.55/user/month) adds AI features as an add-on and 24/7 support.

## Verdict

Both tools are excellent, but they serve different needs. Notion is the better choice for teams that want a modern, AI-powered workspace with flexible databases, custom agents, and automation. It’s ideal for startups, creative teams, and non-technical users who need to build custom workflows without coding. Confluence remains the gold standard for technical teams that rely on Jira and need structured, hierarchical documentation. It’s more cost-effective at scale and offers tighter integration with Atlassian’s suite.

For most teams, Notion’s versatility and AI features provide more value per dollar, especially at the Business tier. However, if your organization is already using Jira or requires strict page hierarchies, Confluence is the safer bet. Evaluate your team’s primary use case—flexibility vs. structure—and choose accordingly.
