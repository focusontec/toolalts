## Overview

Stagewise and Dyad are two open-source tools in the development space, each taking a distinct approach to AI-assisted coding. Stagewise positions itself as an agentic IDE, focusing on creating and orchestrating multiple coding agents that can work together on tasks like app previews and git workflows. It supports a wide range of AI models from different providers, making it a flexible hub for agent-driven development. Dyad, on the other hand, is an AI app builder that emphasizes local execution and zero vendor lock-in. It allows developers to build AI applications entirely on their own machines using their preferred models and tools, with tiered plans for larger projects. Both tools are open source and have similar user ratings, but they cater to different workflows and priorities.

## Pricing

**Stagewise** is completely free with no paid tiers. The entire tool, including all features like agent orchestration, app previews, and git workflow integration, is available at $0. There are no usage limits or credit systems.

**Dyad** offers a freemium model with three tiers:
- **Dyad Free**: $0/month – includes 200 AI credits per month and basic features.
- **Dyad Pro**: $20/month – adds exclusive Pro modes for working on large codebases and likely higher credit limits.
- **Dyad Max**: $79/month – top-tier plan with maximum credits and full access to all features.

## When to Choose Stagewise

Choose Stagewise if your workflow revolves around **orchestrating multiple coding agents** that collaborate on tasks. It is ideal for teams or individual developers who want to automate complex development pipelines, such as generating code, previewing apps, and managing git commits—all within a single IDE. Stagewise’s support for multiple AI models from various providers makes it a strong choice if you need flexibility to switch between models (e.g., GPT-4, Claude, or open-source models) without being locked into one ecosystem. Since it is entirely free, it is also perfect for budget-conscious developers, hobbyists, or open-source projects that need robust agentic capabilities without recurring costs. If your primary need is agent-driven automation and workflow management, Stagewise is the better fit.

## When to Choose Dyad

Choose Dyad if your priority is **building AI applications locally** with full control over your data and models. Dyad is designed for developers who want to avoid vendor lock-in and run everything on their own hardware. Its exclusive Pro modes are a key advantage for working on large codebases, making it suitable for professional developers or teams handling complex, proprietary projects. The tiered pricing (Free, Pro, Max) allows you to scale up as your needs grow, with the Pro plan at $20/month offering a good balance for serious work. If you are building custom AI apps, need local execution for privacy or compliance, and value a straightforward app-building experience over agent orchestration, Dyad is the better choice.

## Verdict

Both Stagewise and Dyad are excellent open-source tools, but they serve different purposes. Stagewise excels as a free, agentic IDE for orchestrating coding agents and automating development workflows, making it ideal for teams that need multi-model support and git integration without any cost. Dyad, with its local-first approach and tiered pricing, is better suited for developers building AI applications who prioritize privacy, large codebase support, and scalability. If you need agent orchestration and workflow automation, go with Stagewise. If you need a local AI app builder with Pro features for big projects, choose Dyad. For most users, the decision comes down to whether you want to manage agents (Stagewise) or build apps (Dyad).
