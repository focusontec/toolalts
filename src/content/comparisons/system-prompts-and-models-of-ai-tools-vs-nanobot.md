## Overview

**system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools** is a comprehensive open-source GitHub repository that aggregates system prompts, internal tools, and AI model details from over 25 popular AI coding assistants, including Cursor, Devin, and Replit. It serves as a reference library for developers and AI enthusiasts who want to study the underlying architectures and prompts of these tools. With over 138k GitHub stars, it has become a highly visible community resource.

**nanobot** is an ultra-lightweight, self-hosted personal AI agent designed to integrate with your workspace, channels, and automation tools. It focuses on executing long-running workflows and is built for developers and power users who want a customizable, on-premise AI assistant. It is open-source under the MIT license and built with Python, supporting multiple large language models (LLMs).

## When to Choose system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools

Choose **system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools** if you are:

- **An AI researcher or enthusiast** who wants to study how popular AI coding assistants (Cursor, Devin, Replit, etc.) are built and prompted.
- **A developer learning prompt engineering** and looking for real-world examples from 25+ tools.
- **Building your own AI coding assistant** and need reference material on system prompts and internal tool architectures.
- **Interested in open-source AI transparency** and want to see the underlying models and prompts used by commercial tools.
- **Not looking for a runnable tool** – this is a reference repository, not an executable agent.

## When to Choose nanobot

Choose **nanobot** if you are:

- **A developer or power user** who wants a lightweight, self-hosted AI agent that integrates with your existing tools and workflows.
- **Looking for automation** – nanobot excels at executing long-running tasks and workflows across channels and workspaces.
- **Privacy-conscious** – since it’s self-hosted, your data stays on your infrastructure.
- **Familiar with Python** and want to customize or extend the agent with multiple LLM backends.
- **Building a personal AI assistant** that can run autonomously and interact with your development environment.

## Verdict

**system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools** and **nanobot** serve fundamentally different purposes despite both being open-source development tools. The former is a static reference library for studying AI tool architectures, while the latter is a dynamic, self-hosted automation agent.

If your goal is **research, education, or understanding how AI coding assistants work**, system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools is the clear choice. Its massive collection of prompts and model details from 25+ tools is unmatched for learning.

If your goal is **practical automation, workflow integration, or having a personal AI agent that runs on your own infrastructure**, nanobot is the better fit. Its lightweight design, MIT license, and focus on long-running tasks make it ideal for developers who want to build custom AI workflows.

For most users, these tools are complementary rather than competing. You could use system-prompts-and-models-of-ai-tools to learn how other assistants are built, then apply those insights when customizing nanobot for your own needs. Both are free, so there’s no cost to exploring either.
